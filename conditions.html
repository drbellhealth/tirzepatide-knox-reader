Who GLP-1 Weight Loss Is For in Knoxville

This is for people with real weight to lose who keep regaining it. Not for five vanity pounds. Below is who it's for. Then a quick note on who shouldn't take it.

Who's a Good Candidate

  • Adults with roughly 20 to 100+ pounds to lose who've stalled with diet and exercise
  • Weight regain after Whole30, Noom, Optavia, intermittent fasting, or repeated dieting
  • Constant 'food noise' — cravings and snacking that willpower hasn't controlled
  • Prediabetes, insulin resistance, or a family history of type 2 diabetes
  • Higher BMI with weight-related issues like sleep apnea, joint load, or blood pressure
  • Patients told by a GP to 'lose 20 pounds first' before being considered for a GLP-1
  • People who want physician supervision rather than a no-screening online vial shop
  • Patients who can commit to monthly phone check-ins for 6 to 12 months
  • Adults pairing weight loss with the practice's other metabolic and recovery services
  • Not appropriate for pregnancy, breastfeeding, or certain thyroid/pancreatic histories (see below)

How Much Weight You Have to Lose

Most people who call have 20 to 100-plus pounds to lose and a pile of failed diets behind them. That's not weak willpower — it's your body fighting to stay heavy, which is what these drugs interrupt. No magic minimum, but they're for real need — usually a BMI around 30, or 27 if you've got something like prediabetes too. A good clinic checks you fit first.

Appetite & Food Noise

First thing you'll notice isn't the scale — it's quiet. The constant food chatter stops. No afternoon cravings, no second plate before you decide. Usually within two to four weeks. People say they just forget to snack. Use that window to lock in protein and better habits.

Plateaus & Dose Adjustments

The scale won't drop in a straight line. Plateaus are normal. Most people lose well for 8 to 12 weeks, then it slows — and that's when the dose goes up at your monthly call. Lots of people hit 15 to 20% of their weight by month six, but it takes a dose bump or two. Never adjusting the dose is why people stall.

Side Effects & What to Expect

Side effects are mostly stomach stuff: nausea, sometimes throwing up, constipation or the runs, heartburn, feeling full fast. Worst right after a dose goes up, then they ease. Smaller meals, eat slow, drink water. Rare serious stuff (pancreas, gallbladder, a thyroid-tumor warning from animal studies) is why a real doctor screens you first.

Protecting Muscle While Losing Fat

Lose weight too fast and some of it's muscle, not fat — and that makes regain easier. So: hit your protein, lift a few times a week, don't crash-diet on top of the shot. At an office that also does the body-and-movement side, you can line up the strength work and keep the muscle that keeps the weight off.

Who Should Not Take GLP-1 Medications

Not for everyone. Pregnant or breastfeeding — no. Family history of medullary thyroid cancer or MEN-2 — no. Active pancreatitis, certain gallbladder or gut problems, some drug combos — those get a hard look. Barely any weight to lose or an eating-disorder history — usually a different plan. The screening call catches all that.
